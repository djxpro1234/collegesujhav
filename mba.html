MBA

MBA Course: Complete Guide to Business & Management Career

MBA (Master of Business Administration) is one of the most popular postgraduate courses for students aspiring to build a career in management, leadership, and business administration. The course develops managerial skills, decision-making abilities, and strategic thinking.

Eligibility Criteria for MBA

To pursue an MBA, candidates must have completed a bachelor’s degree in any discipline from a recognized university with at least 50% marks (45% for reserved categories). Admission is generally based on entrance exams such as CAT, XAT, MAT, CMAT, or state-level exams.

Admission Process

The MBA admission process includes an entrance examination followed by group discussion (GD), written ability test (WAT), and personal interview (PI). Final selection is based on entrance score, academic background, and performance in interviews.

MBA Course Curriculum

The MBA curriculum is designed to provide theoretical knowledge and practical exposure to the business world. It is divided into:

Core Subjects

Core subjects include Marketing Management, Financial Accounting, Human Resource Management, Operations Management, Economics, and Business Law.

Specializations

Students can choose specializations such as:
– Marketing
– Finance
– Human Resource Management
– Operations
– International Business
– Business Analytics

Internship & Projects

MBA programs include summer internships and live projects that provide real-world corporate exposure and practical business experience.

MBA Course Duration & Fees

The duration of the MBA course is typically 2 years. Fees vary depending on the institute, with government colleges charging lower fees compared to private and international business schools.

Career Prospects After MBA

MBA graduates can work as:
– Business Managers
– Marketing Managers
– Financial Analysts
– HR Managers
– Consultants
– Entrepreneurs
